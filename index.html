Australia · Migration · 1984–2025

A Nation Built
on Migration

For four decades, Australia has shaped itself through one of the world's most deliberate immigration programs. This is the story of who came, where from, and why it matters.

01 Overview

40 Years of Australia's
Migration Program

Since 1984, Australia has granted over 5 million visas under its Migration Program. The journey hasn't been linear — recessions, policy shifts, and COVID all left their mark. But one trend is clear: Australia has become increasingly reliant on migration to sustain its population and economy.

02 Composition

Who Do We Let In?
The Shift to Skilled Migration

Australia's migration program wasn't always dominated by skilled workers. In 1984, family migrants outnumbered skilled migrants 4 to 1. Today, the skill stream accounts for nearly 70% of all grants — a deliberate policy shift that reshaped who gets to call Australia home.

03 Source Countries

The Great Reshuffling:
Who Tops the List?

Twenty years ago, the UK was Australia's number one source of migrants. Today, India has taken that crown — and shows no signs of giving it up. This chart tracks the rise and fall of the top 8 source countries from 2004 to 2025, revealing one of the most significant shifts in Australia's migration story.

04 Growth

Twenty Years Apart:
Who Grew, Who Stalled?

Comparing 2004–05 to 2024–25 tells a striking story. Nepal's intake grew by over 2,800% — from just 630 arrivals to nearly 18,500. India and the Philippines more than tripled. The UK barely moved. These aren't just numbers — they reflect shifting global economies, Australia's visa priorities, and where opportunity is perceived to be.

05 Geography

A World Map of
Australia's Migrants

Australia draws migrants from every corner of the globe, but the concentration is striking. South and East Asia now dominate — India, China, the Philippines, Vietnam and Nepal together account for more than half of all arrivals in 2024–25. The old ties to Britain and the Pacific remain, but the centre of gravity has shifted decisively eastward.

06 Intensity

When Did Each Country
Switch On?

This heatmap reveals the intensity of migration from each country over two decades. The dark void of 2020–21 tells the COVID story instantly. But look beyond that — India's row lights up progressively darker year by year. Nepal barely registers in 2004, then explodes. China peaks mid-decade then cools. Every row is a different story.

07 Industry

Which Industries Drive
Skilled Migration?

Australia doesn't just import workers — it imports them for specific industries. Health care and IT have grown steadily as the economy modernises. Accommodation and food surged in the pre-COVID boom then collapsed. COVID hit every sector, but recovery has been uneven — health care and tech bounced back fastest, signalling where Australia sees its future workforce needs.

08 Settlement

Where Do They Settle?
Migration by State

Not all of Australia benefits equally from migration. New South Wales and Victoria absorb more than half of all arrivals between them. Meanwhile, Tasmania and the Northern Territory receive a fraction — raising questions about whether Australia's migration program is reinforcing the dominance of its two largest cities.

09 Visa Categories

Inside the Skill Stream:
How Priorities Have Shifted

The skill stream isn't monolithic — it's made up of distinct visa categories that reflect Australia's changing priorities. Employer Sponsored and Skilled Independent visas once dominated. COVID upended the mix, briefly elevating Global Talent and Business Innovation. Post-COVID, Regional visas surged as Australia tried to push migrants beyond Sydney and Melbourne. In 2024–25, Employer Sponsored clawed back dominance — but Skilled Independent has shrunk dramatically, signalling a shift from individual-led to employer-driven migration.

10 Gender

The Gender Gap in
Skilled Migration

Who gets a skilled visa in Australia depends partly on gender. Business Innovation visas skew heavily male — 72% of recipients are men. Global Talent is similarly imbalanced. But Regional visas are perfectly split, and Partner visas actually favour women. The dashed line marks 50/50 — the further a dot sits from it, the larger the gender imbalance in that visa category.

11 COVID Impact

How COVID Gutted
Australia's Migration Program

When Australia closed its borders in March 2020, the migration program didn't just slow — it collapsed. The red bars show the COVID years. 2019–20 was already falling before borders shut. By 2021–22 grants had dropped to their lowest in a decade. The post-COVID rebound in 2022–23 was record-breaking — pent-up demand unleashed all at once.

12 Citizenship

From Migrant to Citizen:
Who Gets Conferrals?

In 2024–25, Australia conferred citizenship on 165,193 people. This chart traces the full pipeline — from gender, through world region, to country of origin. New Zealand leads with 33,103 conferrals, followed by India at 23,015. But the gender split tells a subtler story: South Asian and Middle Eastern flows skew heavily male, while Pacific & Oceania conferrals — driven by New Zealand — are more balanced. Female recipients account for 53% of all conferrals overall, yet are underrepresented in almost every non-Oceania region.

13 Outcomes

The Long Game:
How Migrants Converge

Fresh off the plane, family stream migrants face a 24% unemployment rate — nearly five times the Australian-born rate of 4.8%. Skill stream migrants start better at 14%, but still struggle early.
